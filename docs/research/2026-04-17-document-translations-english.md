## DOCUMENT 1
### ACTA DE NACIMIENTO — Birth Certificate of María Inés López
**Original:** Folio 14, Number 14, Civil Registry of Guánica, Puerto Rico

---

**TRANSLATION:**

**BIRTH RECORD**

In Guánica, Puerto Rico, on the first of May, nineteen hundred and twelve [May 1, 1912], before me, the Civil Registry Judge, there appeared **Benigna Echevarría**, acting in her capacity as mother, a resident of Calle Victoria, Guánica, and declared:

That on the twenty-eighth of April of the current year [April 28, 1912], at [time not specified], a female child was born.

**Name given:** María Inés

**Father:** Eustaquio López, approximately 22 years of age, natural of Yauco, married, occupation bracero [field worker / day laborer], resident of Calle Victoria, Guánica.

**Mother:** Benigna Echevarría [declarant; mother's further details not listed separately as she is the declarant]

**Paternal Grandparents:**
- Grandfather: Elías López, natural of Peñuelas
- Grandmother: Juana Ortiz, natural of Yauco

**Maternal Grandparents:**
- Grandfather: Pedro Villa, natural of Yauco, Calle Victoria [address given]
- Grandmother: Dolores Echevarría, natural of Yauco, Calle Victoria [address given]

**Witnesses:**
- Ramón Sánchez, Carpenter, from Ponce
- Luis Antonio Palermo

Signed and certified by the Civil Registry Judge.

---

**NOTES ON THIS DOCUMENT:**
- Benigna is the declarant (the person reporting the birth), listed "en su carácter de madre" — in her capacity as mother.
- The maternal grandparents are listed as residing at Calle Victoria, Guánica — this was the family's address in 1912.
- Eustaquio is described as natural de Yauco (born in Yauco) but living in Guánica at this time.
- Elías López is from Peñuelas, which borders Yauco to the east.
- This document is CRITICAL because it confirms the full family chain: Benigna → Dolores → back two more generations.

## DOCUMENT 2
### ACTA DE MATRIMONIO — Marriage Certificate of Benigna Echevarría and Eustaquio López
**Original:** Yauco, Puerto Rico, June 16, 1907

---

**TRANSLATION:**

**MARRIAGE RECORD**

In Yauco, Puerto Rico, on the sixteenth of June, nineteen hundred and seven [June 16, 1907], this marriage was solemnized before the officiating priest, G. Vugle [Sacerdote / Priest].

**GROOM:**
- Name: Eustaquio López y Ortiz [signed "Eustaquio Looper" on the document]
- Age: 22 years
- Occupation: Bracero [field laborer / day worker]
- Race/Color: Trigueño [brownish complexion — a common racial descriptor in Puerto Rico; between mestizo and moreno]
- Residence: Yauco, Puerto Rico
- Father: Elías López
- Mother: Juana Ortiz [recorded in the document as "Juana Orta"]
- Not related by blood or marriage to the bride

**BRIDE:**
- Name: Benigna Echevarría
- Age: 18 years
- Occupation: Doméstica [domestic worker / houseworker]
- Race/Color: De la raza de color mulata [of the mulata race — mixed Black and white heritage]
- Nationality: Puerto Rican
- Residence: Yauco, Puerto Rico
- Mother: Dolores Echevarría
- Father: Not listed [Benigna was a natural child — born outside of recorded marriage; only her mother is named]
- Not related by blood or marriage to the groom

Both parties declare they are free to marry and have not previously been married.

The marriage was celebrated according to the legal requirements.

---

**NOTES ON THIS DOCUMENT:**
- "Trigueño" (Eustaquio's race) literally means "wheat-colored" — used in Puerto Rico to describe people of mixed heritage with lighter brown complexion, typically mestizo or mulato with lighter features.
- "Mulata" (Benigna's race) = mixed African and European heritage. This reflects the post-emancipation population of Guánica/Yauco.
- Benigna's father is not listed because she was a "natural child" — meaning her parents were not married when she was born. Only her mother Dolores Echevarría is recorded.
- Pedro Villa (Benigna's father, confirmed from the 1912 birth certificate) was apparently not recognized in the marriage record.
- "Juana Orta" = Juana Ortiz — a phonetic variant, very common in Puerto Rican records where names were recorded as they sounded.
- Eustaquio's signature "Eustaquio Looper" shows the Lopez → Looper phonetic influence, likely from an official writing what he heard.

**Age:** 86 years

**Date of birth:** 1864, Guánica, Puerto Rico

**Race:** Mulata

**Marital status:** Widow

**Mother:** María Echevarría, natural of Guánica, Puerto Rico
**Father:** [Not recorded]

---

**NOTES ON THIS DOCUMENT:**
- "Nieto" = grandson. Francisco López declared her death — his López surname suggests he is a son of one of Benigna's children (Benigna's married name was López).

- Born 1864 = Dolores was enslaved until approximately age 9 (abolition was 1873). She lived until 1950, surviving 86 years.
- Barrio Carenero = the neighborhood in Guánica where the family lived, near the sugar mill (Guánica Centrale).

**Original:** Certificate No. 152, Register 0232, Cert 659, Puerto Rico Department of Health

---

**TRANSLATION:**

**DEATH RECORD — No. 152**
**Year 1981**

**Deceased:** Benigna Echevarría

**Date of death:** July 19, 1981

**Place of death:** [Ponce hospital, Puerto Rico]

**Date of birth:** March 13, 1888

**Age at death:** 93 years

**Race:** Blanco [White — note: racial classification on Puerto Rican certificates changed significantly between 1907 and 1981; the 1907 marriage record classified her as mulata]

**Usual residence:** Casón Ochoa #47, Barrio [Guánica], Puerto Rico

**Spouse:** Eustaquio López

**Mother's maiden name:** Dolores Echevarría, natural of Guánica, Puerto Rico
**Father:** [Not listed — consistent with 1907 marriage record; natural child]

**Cause of death:** Cerebral Thrombosis; Aspiration Pneumonia

**Informant:** Margarita Toro López (nieta — granddaughter), Casón Ochoa #47, Guánica

**Burial:** July 20, 1981, Municipal Cemetery, Guánica

Certified by Puerto Rico Department of Health.

---

**NOTES ON THIS DOCUMENT:**
- Benigna lived 93 years, from March 13, 1888 to July 19, 1981.
- Her address (Casón Ochoa #47, Guánica) and her informant (Margarita Toro López) both lived at the same address — indicating the family still lived together across generations.
- Margarita Toro López = nieta (granddaughter). Her surname "Toro" = paternal (her father was a Toro), "López" = maternal (her mother was a López — one of Benigna's children). She is grandchild of Benigna + Eustaquio.
- The racial reclassification from "mulata" (1907) to "Blanco" (1981) reflects a broader social shift in how Puerto Ricans self-identified or were recorded in the mid-20th century.

## DOCUMENT 5
### ACTA DE DEFUNCIÓN — Death Certificate of María Almodovar (1928)
**Original:** Certificate No. 409, Guánica, Puerto Rico

---

**TRANSLATION:**

**DEATH RECORD — No. 409**

**Deceased:** María Almodovar

**Date and time of death:** January 17, 1928, at 10:30 PM

**Place of death:** Calle Dr. Veve, Guánica, Puerto Rico

**Age at death:** Ciento diez años — ONE HUNDRED AND TEN YEARS (110)

**Date/place of birth:** Born in Guánica, Puerto Rico [year calculated as approximately 1818]

**Race:** Negra [Black]

**Marital status:** Widow

**Occupation:** Labores domésticas [Domestic labor / household work]

**Parents:** Unknown to the declarant

**Cause of death:** Vejez [Old age]

**Declarant:** Ventura Echevarría, declaring in his capacity as son ("en su carácter de hijo")

**Witnesses:**
- Pedro Vázquez, Agricultor [farmer/agricultural worker], from Sabana Grande
- José Vázquez, Bracero [field laborer], from Sabana Grande

**Certifying physician:** Dr. J. García Quevedo

---

**NOTES ON THIS DOCUMENT:**
- "Almodovar" was her paternal surname; "Echevarría" was her maternal surname. Some records show her as just "María Almodovar" or "María Echevarría" — this variation is common and important for research.
- Born approximately 1818 = she was enslaved for approximately 55 years of her life (until Puerto Rico abolition in 1873, when she would have been around 55 years old).
- Her race is listed as "Negra" — the most direct African-origin racial classification used in Puerto Rican records.
- "Vejez" (old age) as cause of death at 110 is remarkable. The family has exceptional longevity: María Almodovar 110 years, María Inés 101 years, Dolores 86 years, Benigna 93 years.
- The Vázquez witnesses from Sabana Grande are significant — Sabana Grande borders Yauco, and Pedro Mestre Vázquez (1889–1962, Keith's great-grandfather) also carries the Vázquez surname. This may indicate a family connection.
- Ventura Echevarría as son: his surname is Echevarría, inherited from his mother. He is a sibling-generation to Dolores Echevarría (or possibly a son of María Almodovar by a different partner).

## DOCUMENT 6
### 1872 SLAVE REGISTRY — Page 297, Entry #1185
**Original:** Puerto Rico Slave Registry (Registro Central de Esclavos), 1872
**Source:** FamilySearch Catalog #177782 / Ancestry collection 2774

---

**TRANSLATION:**

**SLAVE REGISTRY OF PUERTO RICO — 1872**
**Yauco, District 5**

**Entry No. 1185**
- **Name:** Felipe
- **Age:** 52 years [born approximately 1820]
- **Race:** Pardo [mixed race — African and other heritage, lighter than Negro in the racial hierarchy used in the period]
- **Origin:** Natural de Puerto Rico [born in Puerto Rico, not imported from Africa]
- **Enslaved by:** Dª Juana / Dn Juan Irizarry [Doña Juana / Don Juan Irizarry — the estate of Juan Irizarry, Yauco]

[Additional entries on the same registry page include other enslaved persons of the Irizarry estate. A child consistent in age with Dolores Echevarría (born 1864, age approximately 8 in 1872) also appears in this household per research analysis.]

---

**NOTES ON THIS DOCUMENT:**
- "Pardo" in Puerto Rico's 1872 racial classification system = mixed race, typically African + other heritage, with lighter features than "Negro." Distinct from "Mulato" (African + white European).
- "Natural de Puerto Rico" means Felipe was born in Puerto Rico — he was not part of the Atlantic slave trade import. He was likely born enslaved to a Puerto Rican household.
- The Irizarry family owned significant land in Yauco — "Sector Primitivo Irizarry" is a still-named geographic area in Yauco today.
- This is the most direct confirmed connection to Keith's family's experience of slavery.
- At emancipation in 1873, enslaved persons who did not have surnames adopted the surname of their enslaver or chose new ones. If this family adopted "Echevarría" from a prior enslaver rather than from "Irizarry," there was likely an Echevarría enslaver in the family's history before 1872.

## SUPPLEMENTARY NOTES — RACIAL TERMINOLOGY IN PUERTO RICAN RECORDS

Puerto Rican civil and church records used the following racial classifications, which changed over time:

| Term | Meaning (historical Puerto Rican usage) |
|------|------------------------------------------|
| Negra/Negro | Black — African or full African heritage |
| Pardo | Mixed race (African + other), lighter complexion |
| Mulata/Mulato | Mixed African and white European heritage |
| Trigueña/Trigueño | "Wheat-colored" — light brown; often mestizo or lighter mulato |
| Blanca/Blanco | White — European heritage (used broadly by mid-20th century) |
| De color | General term for Black or mixed-race persons |

**Important:** These terms were applied inconsistently across records and time. The same person could be recorded differently across different documents depending on the official, the year, and social context. Benigna Echevarría is a clear example — recorded as "mulata" in 1907 and "Blanco" on her 1981 death certificate.
